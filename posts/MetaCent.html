Centrism & Metalangauge

In light of support to end the filibuster, Kyrsten Sinema took to the US Senate floor to give a speech ostensibly in the name of protecting democracy. Sinema repeats all the cliche points of protecting the institution, following the American people’s mandate, compromise, warnings against factionalism, etc. Besides some of the worst political theater, the speech is the most distilled form of what Lacan means when he says that ‘there is no metalanguage.’ The basic idea is that one thinks they can discuss a topic totally neutrally without themselves being implicated in any way. In the case of Sinema, she speaks as if she is outside the factionalism and party politics of American democracy, yet her very speech is intrinsic to the phenomena. Her speech is a well-functioning part of it, aids in the perpetuation of the problem itself all the while pretending as if it is the solution and outside of the problem. Sinema says that we must return to these values of compromise and balance in order for a (capital-d) Democratic agenda to succeed, but this belies the fact that it has been her—and her ideological comrade Joe Manchin—which have been the impediment to the agenda. This is the discourse that all centrists engage in. They speak from a supposedly neutral point of measurement, but they don’t question the means of measurement itself. This is the most ideological kind of speech.

All this excluding the fact that the history and majesty we bestow upon institutions like the US Senate are totally false and have always been about power and not some transcendent ideal of democracy.
