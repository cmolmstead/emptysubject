The 'empty subject' names a concept and figure of thought which is above all concerned with minimality, the minimal marking of a place, the minimal instantiation of difference.

The story of the subject across the 20th century is a tortuous one. So many of the philosophies which emerged in the century were above all about exorcism of the subject from theoretical thought. Even philosophies which found themselves on opposite ends of the battlefield did not shake such a commitment—as was the case of Levi-Strauss’ structuralism and Derrida’s deconstruction.
